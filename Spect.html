Measuring Concentration with UV-Vis Spectroscopy

UltraViolet-VISable light spectroscopy uses a laser within the ultraviolet and visible light spectrum of radiation to measure concentrations of compounds in a solution

UV-Vis spectroscopy accomplishes this by using a laser, which is difracted against a crystal prism, passed through a polarized filter that allows for a specific spectrum to be used, then passed through the sample, stored in a container knowns as cuvette

The passthrough is then measured as an absorbance reading by a Charge Coupling Device, and analysed using computer software in accordance to Beer's Law
