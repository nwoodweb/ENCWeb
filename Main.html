* WHAT IS STRUCTURAL BIOLOGY? * 
*******************************

~ Structural Biology is a field of interest within biology that seeks to gain insight i nto the role of shape and composition in biological chemicals

~ These biomolecules often include rather large compounds, known as macromolecules. Macromolecules include

  • DeoxyriboNucleic Acid (DNA) - The genetic code of all living things, and is therefore responsible for most, if not all interactions within all living things
  • RiboNucleic Acid (RNA) - An intermediary compound between the processing of DNA and formation of proteins. Ironically, researchers now believe that RNA predates DNA
  • Proteins, Enzymes, and Peptides - The final result of DNA processing, and is responsible for processing , structure, and regulation within living things. An example being the Keratin helix 6UUI to the right
  • Lipids- Fats, steroids, and some hormones provide structure and regulation

~ These compounds play a crucial role in the survival of living things, and even in the survival of non-living things, such as viruses, viroids (free-floating DNA or RNA), and prions (proteins that damage the structure of other proteins) 

*************************
* COMPUTERS FOR BIOLOGY *
*************************

~ Scientists are constantly unearthing increasing amounts of biological data, leading to the problem of not only storing and sharing this data in a universally accessible manner, but also using this data in a meaningful manner

This has lead to the advent of free to use databases such as the NCBI and UNIPROT , which catalogue a litany of biological data

~ In order to solve increasingly complex biological problems, scientists and doctors make use of High Performance Computing(HPC) and hardware acceleration to expedite the process of solving, or in many cases, approximating, these biological problems 

**************************
* MATHEMATICS IN BIOLOGY *
**************************

~ We use mathematics to make quantifiable sense of our surroundings

~ Mathematics in biology addresses a variety of problems, including biostatistics and public health, chemical bonding, diffusion in the cell, population dynamics, and predator-prey relationships

~ Some of these biological and chemical observations are stochastic, meaning that many of the variables can vary drastically, or in many cases such as the weather, be completely random
